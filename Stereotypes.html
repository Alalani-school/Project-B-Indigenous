Stereotypes

The Red Indian Stereotype

The Red Indian Stereotype was a belief that the Indigenous were referenced with the colour red, which meant blood and was an insult to their culture.
We can see this in play because in this image, it has red as the primary colour.

Savage Indian Stereotype

There was also another belief that the Indgenous were savage and hunted without mercy. That is untrue, as they valued all life and only hunted
what they needed. We can see this in play here as it shows an angry Indigenous (that is, if this person can be classified as Indigenous) holding an axe.

Other Stereotypes For This Image

Some other stereotypes that are not wide-spread but are in this logo are that the Indigenous had blue-black hair, carried axes, and were white. These are all untrue,
as the Indigenous had little to no access to metals until the Europeans came with the Industrial Revolution, they clearly did not have black-blue hair, and they also clearly were not white.
